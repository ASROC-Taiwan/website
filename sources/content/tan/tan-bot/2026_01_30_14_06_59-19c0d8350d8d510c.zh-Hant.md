>
> The observing period is from now to 15 May 2026. We expect to allocate
> approximately 10 observing tracks through this call. Please note that
> the array will remain in the compact configuration throughout the
> entire 2025B semester, providing resolution around 3" at 1.3mm and 2"
> at 870 micron wavelengths.
> Details on the SMA capabilities and status can be found at
> http://sma1.sma.hawaii.edu/status.html.
>
> If you plan to submit a proposal for this supplemental call, please
> inform Hsi-Wei Yen by email at your earliest convenience including
> PI's name and proposal title. We will create a project page for you to
> upload your proposal via the SMA Observer Center
> (http://sma1.sma.hawaii.edu).
>
> The page limit remains 4 pages. However, to facilitate a timely
> process, short and focused proposals (around 2 pages) that simply
> describe the scientific goals, observing strategy, and feasibility are
> encouraged.
>
> The proposal submission deadline is January 30. However, PIs are
> strongly encouraged to submit their proposals as early as possible.
> The SMA-T TAC may review proposals and decide time allocation upon
> receipt, as the semester has started.
